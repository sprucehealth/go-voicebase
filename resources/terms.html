Telemedicine Informed Consent

Telemedicine involves the use of electronic communications to interact with you, consult with a healthcare provider and/or review your medical information for the purpose of diagnosis, treatment, follow-up and/or education. During your telemedicine consultation, details of your medical history and personal health information may be discussed with you or other health professionals through the use of telecommunications technology. In addition, in lieu of a physical examination, your Providers may review photo images or other video or audio recordings of you. Electronic systems used will incorporate network and software security protocols to protect the privacy and security of health information and imaging data, and will include measures to safeguard the data to ensure its integrity against intentional or unintentional corruption.

Anticipated Benefits:
Telemedicine consultations with your Providers through the Spruce Application may have the following possible benefits:

  • improved convenience and access to medical care by enabling you to remain in your location while your healthcare provider may provide care from a distant site; and
  • obtaining expertise of a distant specialist.

Possible Risks:
Medical procedures each present risks, and there are additional potential risks associated with the use of telemedicine. These risks include, but may not be limited to:

  • in rare cases, information transmitted may not be sufficient (e.g. poor resolution of images) to allow for appropriate medical decision making by the Provider;
  • providers available through the Spruce Application may not be able to provide medical treatment or provide for or arrange for any emergency care that may be required;
  • delays in medical evaluation/treatment could occur due to unavailability of providers or deficiencies or failures of the electronic equipment;
  • in rare instances, security protocols could fail, causing a breach of privacy of personal medical information; and
  • in rare cases, a lack of access to all of a patient’s medical records may result in adverse drug interactions or allergic reactions or other judgment errors.
